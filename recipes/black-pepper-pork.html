Phuket Style Black Pepper Pork Belly

How to make Phuket Style Black Pepper Pork Belly

I first experienced this delectable dish at Prai Raya in Bangkok, a renowned restaurant specializing in Southern Thai cuisine. Although I had various thai dishes before, mu hong had somehow eluded my taste buds until I randomly stopped by this restaurant after being fitted for suits at a nearby tailor. Having never had real southern thai style food, I asked the waitress what she recomended and this was the first thing she pointed out. Being a lover of pork belly, I had to try it.

The dish arrived in all its glory, succulent chunks of pork belly, perfectly cooked, bathed in a rich, caramelized gravy infused with bold notes of black pepper. The pork was exceptionally tender, maintaining its structure yet melting in the mouth, with an ideal balance of fat ensuring moist juiciness. This flavor profile leaned towards the savory side, thanks to the prominent black pepper and soy sauce, rather than being overly sweet. I was captivated by its excellence, and ever since, I've been recreating this dish at home. A unique aspect of Thai cuisine is its use of sugar as a preservation technique, so I recommend preparing this dish a day or two ahead of time to allow the flavors to deepen and reach extraordinary heights.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 12 oz pork belly, cut into 1 and 1/2 in cubes
  • 3 tablespoons chopped coriander (cilantro) root or coriander stem
  • 3 tablespoons chopped garlic
  • 2 tablespoons black peppercorns
  • 4 tablespoons rendered pork fat (see below)
  • 5 tablespoons palm or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons black soy sauce
  • 3 star anise, toasted
  • 2 in piece of cassia bark, toasted
  • 1 tablespoon white cardamom pods (about 6 pods), toasted
  • 2 bay leaves, fresh or dried
  • 4 cups pork stock (see below)
  • 3 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 dried mandarin peel (optional, see note)
  • 1 pandan leaf, knotted (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons coriander (cilantro) sprigs, snipped into shorter sprigs of leaf and stem
  • salt
